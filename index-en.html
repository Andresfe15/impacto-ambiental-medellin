Did you know?
Trees can communicate with each other through underground networks of fungi.

Trees can exchange information and nutrients through networks of mycorrhizal fungi that connect their roots, in a process known as "plant communication".

Solar energy can be captured even on cloudy days.

Although clouds reduce the amount of sunlight reaching the Earth, solar panels can still generate electricity on cloudy days thanks to diffuse light.

Plastic can take hundreds of years to degrade in the environment.

Plastic is a material that can persist in the environment for long periods, causing harm to wildlife and polluting ecosystems.

Bees are responsible for pollinating one-third of the food we eat.

Bees play a vital role in the pollination of plants and crops, significantly contributing to food production worldwide.

Tropical forests host a great diversity of plants and animals.

Tropical forests are rich ecosystems that provide habitats for millions of species of plants, animals, and microorganisms.

Frequently Asked Questions

You can reduce your environmental impact at home by adopting practices such as saving energy, recycling, using eco-friendly products, and reducing water and plastic consumption.

You can save energy at home by using efficient appliances, turning off lights and devices when not in use, and improving your home's insulation.

Biodiversity is crucial for maintaining healthy ecosystems and providing environmental services. You can contribute by protecting natural habitats and supporting conservation initiatives.

Materials such as paper, cardboard, plastic, glass, and metal can be recycled. You should properly separate materials and follow your community's recycling guidelines.

Plastics have a negative impact on oceans, marine life, and human health. You can reduce their use by using reusable alternatives and recycling properly.
